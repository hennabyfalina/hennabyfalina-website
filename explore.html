How AI improves festival planning

Our AI models analyze historical booking data, regional preferences, social media trends, and weather patterns to predict which festivals will see the highest demand in your area. This helps artists prepare inventory, block calendars, and recommend the right design styles to clients.

For product sellers, the system forecasts stock requirements by festival type and suggests optimal pricing windows. For customers, it personalizes design recommendations based on occasion, skin tone, and past preferences.

Looking Ahead to 2026

Projected trends & peak seasons

Predictive insights to help you prepare for 2026. These are planning estimates, not confirmed dates or guarantees.

Jan – Feb 2026

Wedding Season Spike

Post-monsoon wedding demand expected to remain strong. Bridal bookings may increase 20–30% compared to 2025. Consider expanding artist network and prepping bridal portfolios.

Mar 2026

Holi & Spring Festivals

Holi 2026 likely falls in mid-March. Expect lighter, minimal designs as traditional intricate patterns may fade during color play. Stock waterproof aftercare products.

Jun – Jul 2026

Eid & Summer Celebrations

Eid ul-Adha projected for mid-June. Arabic and Gulf-style patterns will dominate. Regional festivals in South India (like Kerala Onam prep) may overlap. Plan accordingly.

Oct – Nov 2026

Diwali Peak Season

Diwali 2026 expected late October. This is historically the highest demand period. Corporate bookings, family events, and private parties will peak. Book early or risk missing slots.

Long Weekends 2026

Extended Holiday Bookings

Multiple long weekends in 2026 may lead to destination weddings and extended family gatherings. Track government holiday calendars for opportunistic booking windows.

Regional Events 2026

State-Specific Festivals

Pongal (Tamil Nadu), Baisakhi (Punjab), Onam (Kerala), Durga Puja (Bengal) will see localized spikes. Artists in these regions should prepare region-specific designs and bulk inventory.
